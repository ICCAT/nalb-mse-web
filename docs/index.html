Overview

MSE work for northern albacore started in 2013 (Kell et al., 2013) and began officially with Rec. 15-04. The development of the interim Harvest Controle Rule (HCR) at ICCAT was based on simulations conducted using a specifically designed (MSE) framework. The ICCAT Commission adopted in 2017 (Rec. 17-04), which included an interim harvest control rule for North Atlantic albacore, representing the first harvest control rule adopted by ICCAT. The software for the simulations was reviewed in 2018 (SCRS, 2019). In 2021, the Commission reviewed and adopted the interim HCR in 2021 (Rec. 21-04) as a long-term management procedure, and also adopted the Exceptional Circumstances protocol for North Atlantic albacore in 2021 (Rec. 21-04, Annex 2). The ICCAT Commission has set the TACs since 2017 by the HCR for northern Atlantic albacore (Rec. 17-04, 21-04, and 23-05). The SCRS evaluate the EC every year and ECs have not been invoked since 2022.

Overview

The Management Strategy Evaluation (MSE) process for Northern Albacore (NALB) has evolved through a series of ICCAT Recommendations and SCRS initiatives over more than a decade. The process was initiated following concerns about the sustainability of the stock and the need for more robust, precautionary management frameworks. Rec. 11-13 initiated the rebuilding plan for Northern Albacore, aiming to restore biomass to levels consistent with MSY. Rec. 13-05 called for the SCRS to begin evaluating harvest control rules (HCRs), laying the groundwork for MSE development by requesting advice on the potential use of reference points and control rules for the stock. In 2015, the SCRS identified Northern Albacore as a priority candidate for the application of MSE, noting the availability of data and scientific interest. Rec. 15-04 extended the rebuilding plan and explicitly requested the SCRS to develop HCRs and performance indicators to support the development of MSE. The 2017 stock assessment further motivated this work by confirming the rebuilding trajectory, while also emphasizing the importance of evaluating long-term strategies under uncertainty. From 2017 to 2021, iterative development of Operating Models (OMs), management objectives, and performance metrics progressed under the guidance of the SCRS Albacore Species Group. Early simulation frameworks were tested, and the group coordinated closely with Panel 2 and CPCs to refine priorities. These foundational steps culminated in a formal roadmap toward the adoption of a Management Procedure (MP) through Rec. 21-04,? which set the objective of adopting an MP for Northern Albacore no later than 2023.

Operating Model Development

The Operating Models (OMs) represent a range of plausible dynamics for the Northern Albacore stock. These include uncertainty in: Stock-recruitment relationship (steepness, variability). Natural mortality. Fishery selectivity. Observation error and time-series structure. Initial development and scoping of OMs are being conducted using tools from the openMSE and nalb-mse frameworks. Model conditioning draws on data from the most recent stock assessment and diagnostic testing of retrospective patterns and reference points.

Rec 21-04 Appendix B of Annex 1

The MP shall be applied to set a 3-year constant annual total allowable catch using the following three values estimated from each stock assessment. For each value the median values as reported in the summary table of the SCRS report shall be used: a) The estimate of current stock biomass (BCURR). b) The estimate of the stock biomass at Maximum Sustainable Yield (BMSY). c) The estimate of the fishing mortality at MSY (FMSY).

Principles of ECs

a: When there is evidence that the stock is in a state not previously considered to be plausible in the context of the management strategy evaluation (MSE);

b: When there is evidence that the data required to apply the management procedure (MP) are not available or are no longer appropriate; and/or,

b: When there is evidence that total catch is above the TAC set using the MP.
